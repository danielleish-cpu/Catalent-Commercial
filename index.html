The Situation

Catalent is at an inflection point. The Novo Holdings acquisition created a mandate to grow the business aggressively, with a strategic focus on high-value biologics, GLP-1 programs, and advanced therapies. Capacity freed from facility transfers must now be commercially refilled.

At the same time, Catalent's commercial teams are navigating a familiar tension: deep investments in Salesforce and M365, yet the institutional knowledge that wins deals, prevents missteps, and accelerates proposals still lives in silos across dozens of systems, inboxes, and M&A-inherited platforms.

This document presents an assumption-transparent model for how enterprise AI can accelerate commercial growth, protect margin, and make every existing system investment deliver more value.

Gap Analysis

The Core Problem

Salesforce is Catalent's commercial system of record. But the intelligence that makes commercial teams effective lives across Salesforce, M365, OpenText/Documentum, Eloqua, Gong, Snowflake, and the collective memory of employees across 50+ sites.

No single tool today connects all of these systems with:

  • Permission-aware access - who should see what
  • Custom object support - Real Prospect, not just standard Leads
  • Cross-division context - is this prospect already a customer elsewhere?
  • Actionable agents - not just search, but structured workflows

Key differentiator: Microsoft Copilot's Salesforce connector indexes only 5 standard objects (Accounts, Contacts, Opportunities, Leads, Cases). It cannot see custom objects like Real Prospect, does not connect to OpenText or TrackWise, and operates within the M365 ecosystem only.

The result: commercial teams make decisions with partial information, spend hours assembling context that should be automatic, and occasionally damage customer relationships through blind outreach.

How Glean Helps

Enterprise AI as the Commercial Intelligence Layer

Glean sits between your commercial teams and your existing systems, acting as a context engine that:

  • Indexes everything, respects all permissions: Salesforce (standard + custom objects), M365, OpenText, Eloqua, Gong, Snowflake, and more with source-system permission enforcement
  • Delivers cross-division intelligence at the point of action: Before a rep dials, they know if the prospect is already a major customer in another division, what proposals have been sent, and who internally has the relationship
  • Runs structured agents: Not just Q&A, but automated workflows for daily call prep, post-call CRM updates, proposal drafting, scope-drift alerts, and lead scoring, all grounded in your data
  • Complements Salesforce and M365: Does not replace CRM or productivity tools. Makes them smarter by connecting them to the full enterprise context
